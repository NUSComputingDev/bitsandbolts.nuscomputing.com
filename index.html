The Challenge

Enhancing resilience through IOT enabled with Data Analytics

The threats we face today are ever evolving; increasing in terms of frequency and becoming more complex in nature. Threats such as extreme weather events caused by climate change, terrorist attacks and cybersecurity attacks could cause devastating damages if we are not prepared to handle them.

However, not all is doom and gloom.

Technology has made positive leaps and bounds. In the field of IoT, the amount of data being produced has increased exponentially. It was estimated that 0.1 zettabytes of data in 2013 was from IoT and is expected to grow to a whooping 4.4 zettabytes in 2020.

How can we make better use of all these data to improve our resilience in the areas* of:-

  • Climate Change
  • Public Safety & Security
  • Critical Infrastructure

to create safer and more secure cities?

*Participants are not restricted to these areas

IoT Implementation example:

Resilience against Climate Change

Abnormal weather patterns such as prolonged and heavy rain are becoming increasingly frequent due to climate change. As a result there is an increased likelihood of flooding. By training a model on weather data coupled with rate of increase in water level and speed of water flow, the model will be capable of predicting the probability of a flood occuring in an area. Thereafter early warnings can be issued so that people can take appropriate precautions

Implementation

  • IoT device which can measure water level and speed of water flow will be implemented.

Mockup

  • There will be slides explaining how the data obtained from the IoT implementation will be used to train the model.
  • Graphics illustrating the various functions available in a mobile application which will predict the probability of a flood occurring in a particular area.

Data Analytics Implementation example:

Resilience for Public Safety & Security

Accidents currently require human witnesses to call for emergency response services, which can sometimes be delayed especially in isolated road sections or wee hours.
IoT cameras installed on smart lamp posts will be able to record footage of the roads.
The recorded footage can then be relayed back for real-time video analytics. Models that was built using past footage can be used to determine if a road accident has occurred.
Thereafter, emergency response services can be alerted to a possible accident, increasing promptness of response.

Implementation

  • Lightweight and low-latency edge computer vision algorithms which can detect road accidents.
  • Data pipeline for model training and inference.

Mockup

  • Slides explaining and illustrating innovative geo-distributed video analytics infrastructure and/or features of smart iot cameras which enable effective detection of road accidents.
